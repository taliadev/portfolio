Resume

What do you do when faced with a pile of old laptops with no power cords? The answer, it turns out, is simple: loot them for their displays and make a “magic mirror”.


I spent the first semester of my sophomore year of high school in a makerspace class doing just that—meticulously disassembling laptops, 3D printing frame brackets, cutting down a one-way mirror, and programming a Raspberry Pi. The goal: to create a mirror that would show not only my reflection but also the time, weather, and upcoming Google Calendar events.




Unfortunately, I couldn't just work on one project the entire semester. Especially not while waiting for parts to ship in from China. So, I spent my free time embroidering, making origami lamps, and defacing the Internet:




All of these projects are playfully framed within the design thinking process on my blog.
